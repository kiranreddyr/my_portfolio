Five hundred orders.
One queue.
No idea which were burning.

05 · Product Strategy

Augment the manager.
Don't replace them.

A data scientist on every manager's shoulder — surfacing the right risks at the right time, so they act instead of react.

Pillar i.
Predict

Prediction over detection

Every feature must create intervention opportunity. If it can't give 4h of lead time, it's a report, not a product.

Pillar ii.
Augment

Reduce friction, not control

Automation must feel like the system is working for them. Override stays one tap away — always.

Pillar iii.
Compound

Every action trains the model

Accept, override, flag — each is a labelled signal. The flywheel is the long-term moat.

09 · Tradeoffs

The calls that defined the product.

4h refresh, not real-time?

Streaming infra was 5× cost. 4h still gave 8–20h lead on 75+ scores. Reserved 1h cadence for top risks only.

Opt-out auto pickslip?

Opt-in plateaus at 30%. Default = better behavior. 85%+ adoption was the dividend.

No per-manager override data to leadership?

Surveillance kills overrides. Overrides are the training signal. Trade visibility for data quality.

Pilot in worst sites, not average?

Needed signal strong enough to be undeniable. Average sites = invisible improvement.

Embed in existing screens?

New apps require new habits. Embedded = unavoidable. Constraint forced the right design.

Defer cross-warehouse to P3?

Marketplace problem on top of fulfillment. Phase 1–2 already justified the build.

11 · Reflections

What I'd tell my past self.

What worked

Framing AI as amplification, not replacement. Removed the biggest adoption barrier in one decision.

What I'd change

Build override capture in the MVP. Phase 1 training signals were lost forever — the richest data we had.

AI PM lesson

Trust is fragile and non-linear. One bad week undoes three good months. Guardrails matter more than you think.

Stakeholder lesson

The Ops Head was the critical path. Should have shipped their dashboard earlier — would have saved 4–6 weeks.

Open question

Will the unserviceable model generalize across new supplier networks? That's the test for scale.

Final takeaway

By Month 4, managers stopped saying "the AI." They said "the queue." Invisibility is the highest form of product success.
