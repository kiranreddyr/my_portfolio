The Problem

Warehouse operations were paper-based

Across the distributor network, goods arrival processing was manual and decoupled from the WMS. GRNs were still paper-based or loosely integrated, creating a coordination nightmare: paper receipts, manual data entry, picking instructions printed on sheets, and no real-time visibility into completion status.

Pickers didn't have mobile access to picking lists. Instead, they relied on printed pickslips that became outdated as orders changed. Warehouse managers spent hours on manual coordination: chasing pickers for status updates, reprinting pickslips when orders were cancelled, and manually tracking completion.

Key Insight

The core inefficiency wasn't warehouse capacity: it was process fragmentation. Every step (GRN, prioritization, picking, tracking) was disconnected, creating a cascade of rework and coordination overhead. Integration was the key to unlocking productivity.

Design Thinking

Empathize, define, ideate

Empathize

Warehouse Operations Pain

Observed manual GRN processes taking 45+ minutes per receipt, pickers waiting for instructions, and managers doing manual status tracking. Warehouse staff felt like coordinators, not operators.

Define

Problem Statement

How might we digitize warehouse operations end-to-end, from GRN to picking to completion, to eliminate manual coordination and give pickers real-time actionable instructions?

Ideate

Solution Approach

Three models considered: mobile-first from scratch (startup-like, high risk), desktop-first (slow adoption), and integration-first (leverage existing WMS). Chose integration-first with progressive mobile enablement.

Validate

Pilot & Rollout

Piloted with 5 beta distributors, validated QR-based GRN and mobile picking reduced processing time by 50%. Phased rollout to 90+ distributors over 8 months.

Product Thinking

Strategic decisions that shaped the product

Why mobile-first for pickers? Desktop picking isn't feasible in a warehouse. Pickers are constantly moving. A mobile app meant pickers could receive instructions on the floor, mark completion in real-time, and eliminate the manual status chasing that consumed managers' time.

QR code strategy: Rather than overhaul the entire GRN process, we added QR codes to existing receipt documents. A simple scan unlocked digital workflows, reducing adoption friction and allowing distributors to phase in digitization at their own pace.

Change management challenge: Warehouse staff had been doing manual processes for years. Success required focusing on tangible benefits they could feel immediately: fewer reprinted pickslips, fewer manager interruptions, real-time completion visibility. We made the shift feel like relief, not disruption.

Phased Rollout

Regional Waves

Rather than big-bang rollout, deployed region by region. Early waves informed training for later regions, reducing adoption friction significantly.

Success Metric

Processing Time

Primary north star: reduce goods inbound-to-picking processing time from 45 minutes to under 25 minutes per receipt by month 3.

Technical Approach

How the integration works

  • QR-Based GRN Digitization: Added QR codes to receipt documents that, when scanned, create digital GRN records in the WMS instantly, eliminating manual data entry and paper shuffling
  • Service-Level Driven Pickslip Automation: Integrated SLA risk scores with picking algorithms to auto-generate batched pickslips that respect service level priorities while optimizing picker efficiency
  • Mobile Picker App: Built a mobile-optimized app that gives pickers real-time picking instructions, tracks completion in real-time, and syncs back to the WMS instantly
  • Manager Dashboard: Created a real-time completion tracking dashboard that gives warehouse managers visibility into picking progress without manual status chasing
Impact & Results

Cut processing time in half

50% reduction in goods processing time (45→22 minutes per receipt) and 40-60% reduction in manual coordination overhead. Warehouse managers shifted from reactive firefighting to proactive planning. Pickers had clear, real-time instructions and saw completion reflected instantly in the system.

The Bigger Picture

This wasn't just a speed play: it unlocked a feedback loop. Real-time data from picking operations informed better future prioritization, smarter pickslip batching, and continuous improvement cycles that kept productivity gains compounding month over month.
