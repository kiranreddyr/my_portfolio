The Problem

Dead stock is locked capital

Distributors across India were sitting on slow-moving and dead stock worth crores. Parts weren't selling in their region but were in demand elsewhere. There was no systematic way to match surplus inventory with demand across the network.

The core issue wasn't lack of demand: it was visibility. Distributor A had surplus of a part that Distributor B urgently needed 200km away, but neither knew about the other's situation.

Key Insight

The problem wasn't inventory: it was information asymmetry. Every distributor was operating in isolation, making local decisions without network-level intelligence.

Design Thinking

Empathize, define, ideate

Empathize

Distributor Pain Points

Field visits to 12+ distributors revealed: warehouse space consumed by dead stock, capital locked for 6-18 months, no mechanism to liquidate beyond deep discounts to local retailers.

Define

Problem Statement

How might we enable distributors to convert non-moving inventory into revenue by connecting surplus with demand across the national network?

Ideate

Solution Concepts

Explored 3 models: central buyback, peer-to-peer marketplace, and AI-matched exchange. The marketplace model won with lowest operational overhead and highest network effects.

Validate

Pilot Approach

Tested with 8 distributors in South India first. Validated demand-supply matching accuracy and distributor willingness to transact laterally.

Product Thinking

Strategic decisions that shaped the product

Why a marketplace over central buyback? A buyback model requires TVS to take on inventory risk. The marketplace model creates a self-sustaining ecosystem where distributors trade directly. TVS provides the intelligence layer, not the capital.

Pricing algorithm philosophy: The discount engine balances three forces: seller's holding cost (incentive to sell), buyer's demand urgency (willingness to pay), and network-level price consistency (brand protection). We optimized for transaction velocity, not margin maximization.

Trust and adoption: Distributors are competitive by nature. Anonymous listing with quality guarantees was critical to overcome the "why would I help my competitor" objection.

Build vs Buy

Custom-Built

No off-the-shelf solution existed for B2B lateral inventory exchange in automotive. Built ground-up on top of existing DMS infrastructure.

Success Metric

Dead Stock %

Primary north star: reduce dead stock as % of total inventory from 18% to under 14% within 6 months of launch.

Technical Approach

How the AI engine works

  • Demand-Supply Analysis: Built algorithms to identify non-moving parts at each distributor and match them with active demand signals from other locations
  • Dynamic Discount Optimization: Created pricing models that calculate optimal discounts to incentivize cross-distributor purchases while preserving margins
  • Marketplace UX: Designed a self-serve platform where distributors can browse available surplus inventory, filtered by relevance and proximity
  • Proximity Intelligence: Logistics cost factored into matching. Closer distributors get priority to minimize transfer overhead
Impact & Results

Converting locked capital into active revenue

Projected 20% reduction in dead stock across the network, converting locked capital into active revenue. The marketplace creates a win-win: sellers liquidate stale inventory, buyers get parts at competitive prices with reduced lead times.

The Bigger Picture

This isn't just an inventory tool: it's a network intelligence layer. Every transaction generates data that improves demand prediction, informs procurement strategy, and strengthens the overall distributor ecosystem.
