The Problem

Distributors didn't know they were underperforming

Most distributors in the network operated in a data vacuum. They tracked their own metrics but had no visibility into how they compared against peers. A distributor with 78% service level thought they were doing okay — they didn't know the network average was 85% and the best-in-class was 92%.

This information asymmetry meant no healthy competitive pressure or peer learning. Distributors that were underperforming weren't even aware of it. There was no mechanism for the network to learn from best practices or for laggards to understand where they were falling short.

Key Insight

Performance is relative. Without peer visibility, distributors can't benchmark progress or learn from network-wide best practices. Transparent performance data — especially anonymous — creates healthy competition and drives continuous improvement across the ecosystem.

Product Thinking

Strategic decisions that shaped the product

Why anonymous benchmarking? If distributors knew "you're worse than Distributor X," it could create defensiveness. Anonymous comparison eliminates ego and focuses conversations on performance against the network, not against individuals. It's about "how do we reach top quartile" not "why are we losing to them."

Privacy considerations: Built strict data governance — no distributor can see another distributor's data, only aggregate statistics and rankings. This protected competitive sensitivity while enabling benchmark transparency.

Gamification elements: Top performers got recognition; underperformers got targeted AI recommendations. This created positive reinforcement cycles — visible progress motivated continued effort. Monthly "KPI highlights" reports celebrated improvements across the network.

Data Trust

Privacy-First Design

No distributor can identify peers from data alone. All comparisons are anonymized, with access controls preventing data leakage. Built trust through transparent data governance.

Success Metric

Network Improvement

Primary north star: increase network average service level from 85% to 88%+ through peer-driven continuous improvement cycles within 6 months.

Technical Approach

How the benchmarking engine works

  • Real-Time KPI Aggregation: Built ETL pipelines that aggregate KPI data from 90+ distributors hourly, creating real-time views without compromising privacy or performance
  • Anonymous Peer Comparison: Created ranking and quartile systems that let distributors see how they compare without revealing peer identities, using statistical aggregates and anonymized cohorts
  • Root Cause Analysis Engine: Developed AI models that correlate performance gaps with operational factors (days pending, unserviceable rate, GRN velocity) to suggest actionable root causes
  • Personalized Recommendation System: Built recommendation engine that surfaces best practices from top-performing distributors and tailors them to each distributor's specific gaps
Impact & Results

Transformed culture to data-driven improvement

90+ distributors now have real-time KPI visibility and peer benchmarking access. Within 6 months of launch, 75% of underperforming distributors had closed their gap by at least 2%, demonstrating the power of transparent performance data and peer learning.

The Bigger Picture

This wasn't just a reporting tool — it became the foundation for a data-driven culture shift. Distributors started having conversations: "What are top quartile distributors doing differently?" and "How do we reach their performance level?" The network lifted together.
