i. The Problem 01 / 09

Each warehouse was an island.
The cost of silence was paid in crores.

A part aged twelve months in Chennai was the same part a Hyderabad service desk turned a customer away for. Neither knew. The information lived in spreadsheets that never spoke to each other.

1
Capital, parked.
Crores held in parts aging six to eighteen months. No liquidation path. Quarterly write-offs treated as the cost of doing business.
2
Service, refused.
Distributors turning customers away for the very parts sitting on a shelf 200 km away. Revenue lost to nothing but distance.
3
Information, asymmetric.
Not procurement. Not pricing. The whole network was already solvent — it just couldn't see itself.

ii. Discovery 02 / 09

I went to the warehouses.
The signal was already there.

Three weeks in the field before a single line of PRD. The clearest evidence wasn't in dashboards — it was in the workarounds people had invented to survive without us.

Method 01 · Ethnographic
Twelve warehouse floors, walked.
Color-coded sticker systems for aging stock. Quarterly write-off rituals. Managers who knew exactly which 200 SKUs were going to embarrass them — and had no recourse.
12 sites · 3 weeks · South India
Method 02 · Quantitative
Eighteen months of inventory, read.
63% of dead stock concentrated in 15% of SKUs. The pareto was extreme — and it gave us a precise "start here" for the matching engine.
90 distributors · 50,000+ SKUs
Method 03 · Behavioral
The phone calls, listened to.
Regional sales reps were already asking informally — "do you have part X?" — and getting lucky. The behavior existed. We didn't need to invent it. We needed to give it a channel.
The deciding signal
The Insight
When people invent their own broken solution, the problem is real. We weren't creating behavior. We were giving existing behavior a proper channel.

iv. The AI Question 04 / 09

Not can AI do this.
Should it.

Four candidates. Three earn it. One stays deterministic — because not everything wants to be a model.

Earns AI
Demand-supply matching
50K SKUs × 90 nodes × real-time signals. Rule-based comparison is O(n²) with no scoring. ML scales to sub-second and improves with each match.
Earns AI
Dynamic pricing
Three forces in tension — holding cost, urgency, brand consistency. Multi-objective optimization is what models are for.
Earns AI
Dead-stock prediction
Flag a SKU 30 days before it crosses the threshold. Needs aging patterns, seasonality, model-lifecycle. A threshold can't see this.
Stays simple
Settlement & payouts
Fixed contractual rules between distributors. Deterministic logic. Adding ML adds latency and unpredictability for zero gain.

vi. Strategy 06 / 09

Three pillars.
One flywheel.

Network for liquidity. Trust for adoption. Intelligence for compounding. Pull any one out and the marketplace stalls.

Pillar 01
Network as the moat.
Each node added makes every other node more useful. The defensibility is in the relationships TVS already has — and nowhere else.
Pillar 02
Trust as the foundation.
Anonymous listings remove the deepest fear — exposing weakness to a competitor. TVS guarantees the part. Identity becomes optional.
Pillar 03
Intelligence as the flywheel.
Every transaction sharpens the next. Match quality, pricing, demand forecasting — all improve with use. The advantage compounds.

ix. Field Notes 09 / 09

What I'd tell past me
on Day 1.

What worked
Field before PRD.
Three weeks in warehouses gave us conviction the demand was real. The phone-call workaround was the only research finding I needed.
What I'd redo
Logistics, sooner.
The AI was ready three weeks before the physical fulfillment was. Friction killed Month-1 transactions that didn't need to die.
AI lesson
The model isn't the risk.
Adoption is. We added human review for the first 30 days — a wrong price could have damaged brand trust permanently.
Stakeholder lesson
Sell the not-doing.
The hardest pitch was convincing finance we should not buy back inventory. The marketplace model felt counterintuitive — until the unit economics made it obvious.
Open question
Where else does this work?
Spare parts → finished vehicles? What's the minimum density before liquidity self-sustains? Worth the next memo.
PM takeaway
Lead with the outcome.
"We built an AI marketplace" is a feature. "We freed crores of locked capital" is the story. The AI explains it. It isn't the headline.
End of case

The network was always whole.
It just needed to hear itself.
