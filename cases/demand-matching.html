The Problem

Inventory was locked in silos

The distributor network operated as independent nodes. When a customer in Mumbai needed a part that Distributor A didn't stock, they faced a stockout and delay. Meanwhile, 80km away in Thane, Distributor B had surplus stock of that same part sitting idle.

There was no visibility into network-wide supply. Each distributor only knew their own inventory. Customers experienced frustrating wait times and cancellations, while the network as a whole had sufficient inventory. It was just in the wrong places. This represented massive lost revenue and customer dissatisfaction.

Key Insight

The network had a distribution problem, not a supply problem. By connecting demand signals from one distributor with surplus inventory at another, we could optimize the entire network's fulfillment velocity and reduce customer wait times dramatically.

Product Thinking

Strategic decisions that shaped the product

Why AI-powered matching? Manual broker systems are slow and expensive to scale. By automating the matching logic, we could surface opportunities in real-time. The algorithm considers: demand urgency, inventory proximity, logistics cost, and historical conversion probability.

Logistics cost optimization: A nearby distributor can transfer faster and cheaper than a distant one, even if the distant one has surplus. Our matching algorithm factors distance, transit time, and transfer cost into recommendations. Some transfers are economically viable, others aren't.

Trust between competitors: Distributors are naturally competitive. We solved this by making transfers mutually beneficial: the supplying distributor gets the cost of goods + agreed margin (no loss), the demanding distributor fills a customer order that would otherwise be lost, and TVS captures data. Everyone wins.

Network Effects

Scale Advantage

The more distributors in the network, the more opportunities for matches. This creates a virtuous cycle: bigger network means more matches, which means higher fulfillment rates, which means more value for members.

Success Metric

Network Fulfillment

Primary north star: reduce network-wide stock-out duration through lateral transfers. Secondary: increase overall parts availability from 88% to 92%+.

Technical Approach

How the matching engine works

  • Demand-Supply Matching: Built real-time pipelines that detect unmet customer demand and correlate with available inventory across the distributor network
  • Logistics Optimization: Created algorithms that balance transfer cost (distance, urgency) against demand urgency and conversion probability to recommend only economically viable transfers
  • Predictive Demand Modeling: Developed models that forecast demand spikes and pre-position inventory at strategic distributor nodes to minimize future transfer needs
  • Trust and Incentive Alignment: Designed pricing mechanism where supplying distributor receives cost + margin, preventing loss of income while enabling network-wide fulfillment optimization
Impact & Results

Transformed network fulfillment efficiency

Demand-supply matching unlocked lateral stock transfers across distributors, reducing fulfillment gaps and enabling faster customer delivery. In the pilot, 200+ successful lateral transfers were completed, recovering 15% of would-be lost sales and reducing average fulfillment delay by 3-4 days.

The Bigger Picture

This demonstrated the power of network intelligence. By treating the distributor network as a connected system rather than isolated nodes, we unlocked significant optimization opportunities. Future evolution will add demand prediction to pre-position inventory and further reduce transfer overhead.
