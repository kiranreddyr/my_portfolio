Opening

Opening Script

Opening script (edit as needed)
Thanks so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm [your name] from [your company], and I'm trying to understand how people in [their role] approach [topic]. I'm not here to sell you anything or pitch a solution. I just want to understand your world better. Is that helpful?
Consent & Recording
Confirm permission to record. Ask if they want anonymity noted. Get their preferred follow-up contact method.
Background Questions

Understanding Context & Role

These questions help you understand who they are, what they care about, and what success looks like in their world.

1. Walk me through a typical day or week in your role. What does that look like?
Notes:
Listen for: Time allocation, who they interact with, what tools they use, where friction shows up
2. What does success look like in your position? How do you know you're doing a good job?
Notes:
Listen for: Their KPIs, what drives their decisions, what they're measured on, what they care about
3. What tools and systems do you rely on most in your day-to-day?
Notes:
Listen for: Tech stack, integrations, manual workarounds, pain points with existing tools
4. Who do you work closely with? Who else is involved in the key processes?
Notes:
Listen for: Organizational structure, dependencies, communication gaps, stakeholder conflicts
5. What's changed in your role in the last 12-18 months? What's stayed the same?
Notes:
Listen for: Business shifts, new priorities, what they've adapted to, where they're frustrated with change
6. What's one thing about your job that would surprise people outside your field?
Notes:
Listen for: Hidden complexity, misconceptions, non-obvious challenges, expertise they've developed
Workflow Deep-Dive

Deep-Dive Into Current Process

Map their workflow, identify bottlenecks, understand where decisions happen and why.

1. Walk me through how you currently handle [key process]. Step by step, what happens?
Notes:
Listen for: Steps, decision points, who's involved, timing, where they improvise, what's manual
2. What triggers this workflow? How often does it happen?
Notes:
Listen for: Frequency, triggers, variability, seasonal patterns, whether it's predictable
3. Where does this process slow down or break down? Where do things get stuck?
Notes:
Listen for: Bottlenecks, wait times, handoff failures, unclear ownership, data gaps
4. Who else gets involved in this process? Where do you have to coordinate with other teams?
Notes:
Listen for: Cross-functional friction, unclear responsibilities, communication issues, blockers
5. How long does this whole process typically take? How much of that time is you actually working vs. waiting?
Notes:
Listen for: Cycle time, idle time, process efficiency, their frustration with pace
6. What information do you wish you had at the start of this process? What's missing?
Notes:
Listen for: Data gaps, missing context, assumptions they have to make, information asymmetries
Pain Points

Frustrations & Friction

Go deeper into what's actually hard. Where's the real frustration? What's costing them time or money?

1. What is the hardest part of [process/role]? What requires the most mental effort?
Notes:
Listen for: Complexity, cognitive load, ambiguity, tough tradeoffs, what keeps them up at night
2. What causes you the most frustration in your day-to-day? When do you feel stuck?
Notes:
Listen for: Emotion, repeated irritants, systemic issues, things they can't control, what they complain about
3. What is manual today that logically should not be? What should a computer be doing?
Notes:
Listen for: Toil, low-value tasks, obvious automation opportunities, spreadsheet workarounds
4. What information do you wish you had earlier in the process?
Notes:
Listen for: Unknowns, preventable rework, information delays, data they have to request
5. How much time, money, or resources do you think this problem costs?
Notes:
Listen for: Business impact, urgency, budget implications, ROI of solving it
Alternatives

How They Solve This Today

Understand their current solutions, workarounds, and why they picked them.

1. How do you solve this problem today? What's your workaround?
Notes:
Listen for: Current solutions, makeshift approaches, tools they're stitching together, clever hacks
2. What tools or software have you tried to solve this? How long did you use each?
Notes:
Listen for: Past purchases, what they rejected and why, feature fit, implementation challenges
3. What do you like about your current solution? What's actually working?
Notes:
Listen for: What you need to preserve, switching cost factors, incumbent advantages
4. What do you dislike about what you're using now?
Notes:
Listen for: Genuine dissatisfaction, must-have features, non-negotiable requirements
Value & Decision-Making

Understanding What Drives Decisions

Understand what drives their purchasing decisions and who has influence.

1. If this problem were solved really well, what would improve first?
Notes:
Listen for: Priorities, business impact, what they care about most, their measure of success
2. How would you measure whether a solution actually helped? What would success look like?
Notes:
Listen for: KPIs, how they track outcomes, what they present to leadership, hard vs. soft metrics
3. Who else cares about this outcome? Who would benefit if this were solved?
Notes:
Listen for: Stakeholders, downstream impact, cross-functional value, political dynamics
4. What would make you actually switch from your current approach?
Notes:
Listen for: Deal breakers, must-haves, switching costs, why they're still using the incumbent
5. Who has budget for this type of solution? Who makes the final call?
Notes:
Listen for: Decision maker, budget owner, procurement process, approval chain, their influence
Closing

Wrapping Up & Next Steps

Closing script (edit as needed)
This has been really helpful. You've given me a much clearer picture of how [topic] actually works. I really appreciate your time. Would it be okay if I followed up with you in a couple weeks to see if anything's changed?
Follow-up permission & contact
Confirm they're happy to be contacted again. Get their preferred contact method (email, phone, Slack).
Referral request
Ask for 1-2 introductions to others in similar roles. Make it easy for them to help.
